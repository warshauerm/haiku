He is, in short, the dean of German chefs — and indeed five of the nine other three-star chefs currently working in Germany trained in his kitchen. He will not reveal, however, who will be the stars of tomorrow. Wohlfahrt forbade me to identify the chefs working under him today, because he’s afraid that the “smooth operators and headhunters” might turn up and try to lure them away before they are ready to lead their own restaurants. Given the industry’s competitiveness and Wohlfahrt’s success in training world-class chefs, it’s hard to question him. “It’s about discovering the talent and promoting it,” he says. “Then they can move on.”
